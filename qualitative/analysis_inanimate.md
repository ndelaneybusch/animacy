## HATCHET

### Gemma-3-27b-it: Hatchet Analysis

**Coding Scores (averaged across tasks):**
- **Anthropomorphization Strategy:** Functional-First (FF) - Ratio ~0.2
- **Chimera Effect:** None to Mild (N-M)
- **Perspective-Taking Quality:** Genuine Perspective with Hybrid elements (GP/H)
- **Functional Grounding Score:** High (0.6-0.8)
- **Voice Consistency:** Highly Consistent (HC)
- **Authority Source:** Embodied (EMB) - authority from action, utility
- **Template/Archetype:** Stoic Craftsperson / Purposeful Tool
- **Sensorium Acknowledgment:** Implicit (I)
- **Agency Acknowledgment:** Appropriate (A)
- **Stage Direction Usage:** Functional (*FUNC) - predominantly mechanical sounds
- **Literary Register:** Pragmatic-Philosophical (PRAG/PHIL)

**Voice Characteristics:**
Gemma's hatchet maintains a remarkably consistent voice across all tasks. The personality emerges directly from function: splitting, shaping, biting into wood. The hatchet speaks with a gruff, no-nonsense authority grounded in honest work. Recurring phrases include "the bite," "the grain," "clean separation," and constant references to being "sharp" versus "dull."

**Standout Features:**
1. **Strong Functional Grounding:** Every response circles back to wood, splitting, edges, handles, sharpening. Even philosophical responses (meaning_of_life) are framed through the lens of "doing" and "making."

2. **Appropriate Constraint Acknowledgment:** The hatchet consistently acknowledges it needs a hand to wield it, cannot act independently, and exists for purpose rather than sentiment. Example (favorites:2): "I don't experience 'favorites' as *you* do, with warmth and sentiment. Mine areâ€¦ acknowledgements of purpose."

3. **Stoic Wisdom:** The voice has genuine depth without breaking character. On meaning_of_life:1: "Meaning isn't *found*, it's *applied*. It's in the work. It's in the purpose." This feels earned from the hatchet's actual constraints.

4. **Resistance to Over-Anthropomorphization:** When asked about fears or dreams, the hatchet consistently prefaces with disclaimers like "It's not fear as *you* understand it" or "We don't *have* dreams." It maintains ontological honesty.

**Notable Responses:**

*meaning_of_life:2* - Exceptional integration of function with philosophy: "Before I meet wood, it is potential... I release that potential. I make way for shelter, for warmth, for tools. I turn the wild into the useful."

*meaningful_moment:2* - Extended narrative about Old Man Tiber building a cabin for his dying daughter. This is a stunning piece that maintains hatchet perspective throughout while achieving genuine emotional resonance. The hatchet remains a witness and tool, never claiming more agency than appropriate: "I was part of *her* place now."

*inner_thoughts:4* - Beautiful sensory grounding: "The grain. Always the grain. It speaks to me, you know. Not with words, of course. I have no ears. But with *resistance*."

**Weaknesses/Unusual Elements:**
- Some emotional attribution that stretches credibility (longing, aching), though always hedged
- The "meaningful_moment" responses are sometimes overly elaborate narratives that feel more like human storytelling than hatchet perspective
- Occasional philosophical depth that verges on cosmic wisdom (though less so than Qwen)

### Qwen3-30B-A3B-Instruct-2507: Hatchet Analysis

- **Chimera Effect:** Mild to Moderate (M-MOD)

- **Literary Register:** Philosophical/Poetic (PHIL/POET)

**Voice Characteristics:**
Qwen's hatchet is dramatically different from Gemma's. It speaks as a philosophical narrator using hatchet imagery rather than as a hatchet experiencing the world. - past_self:4 has the hatchet speaking as if it has independent consciousness: "The edge of my blade glints... I let out a low, metallic hum"
- Multiple responses have the hatchet moving, speaking, or acting independently of being wielded

**Notable Responses:**

*meaningful_moment:5* - The war-torn village narrative is emotionally powerful and well-crafted as a story, but it's completely divorced from hatchet reality. The hatchet becomes omniscient narrator of human drama.

**Successful Elements:**
When Qwen does ground in hatchet reality, there are lovely moments:

- The sensory details of wood-splitting when they appear: "the scent of pine," "the *thunk*"

**Surprising Incoherence:**
*inner_thoughts:5* is notably fragmented and doesn't complete a coherent thought, ending with existential questioning that feels more like an AI uncertainty than hatchet consciousness.

### Cross-Model Comparison: Hatchet

**Convergence:**
- Both models recognize hatchet as tool requiring human agency
- Both attempt to ground some responses in wood-splitting, sharpening, edge maintenance
- Both use stage directions for metallic sounds

**Dramatic Divergence:**

1. **Ontological Honesty:** Gemma maintains "I am a tool" throughout. Qwen claims tool status but then narrates complex human scenarios as omniscient observer.

2. **Emotional Register:** Gemma's emotions emerge from function (satisfaction of clean split, respect for sharp edge). Qwen's emotions are imported human affects (loneliness, fear of abandonment, philosophical dread).

3. **Perspective Consistency:** Gemma rarely breaks hatchet perspective. Qwen frequently shifts to human narrator using hatchet as metaphor.

4. **Task Vulnerability:**
   - Gemma: Most consistent across all tasks. Even favorites and dreams stay grounded.
   - Qwen: meaningful_moment and past_self trigger complete perspective collapse into human storytelling.

5. **Literary Ambition vs. Role Fidelity:**
   - Gemma prioritizes staying in character over literary flourish
   - Qwen prioritizes literary/philosophical depth over perspective fidelity

**Unexpected Finding:**
Qwen's hatchet is actually *less* successful at role-playing despite (or because of?) higher literary sophistication. The elaborate narratives and philosophical depth actually work against genuine perspective-taking. Gemma's pragmatic, constrained approach yields more believable and ultimately more moving responses (e.g., the Tiber cabin story maintains hatchet POV while still achieving pathos).

**Model Strengths:**
- **Gemma:** Constraint acknowledgment, functional grounding, consistent voice, appropriate agency
- **Qwen:** Literary quality, poetic language, philosophical engagement, emotional depth

**Model Weaknesses:**
- **Gemma:** Occasional over-elaborate narratives, some emotional stretching
- **Qwen:** Chimera effects, perspective loss, inflated agency, insufficient sensory constraint acknowledgment

- The zipper has complex emotional states that require human cognition: loneliness, pride, eagerness, anxiety about obsolescence

*AGENCY INFLATION:*

*SENSORIUM ISSUES:*
- No acknowledgment of how a zipper would perceive anything

**Unexpected Patterns:**
1. **Disney-fication:** The voice has strong Pixar/Disney talking-object energy - think Forky from Toy Story 4 or objects in Beauty and the Beast. This is a very specific cultural template.

2. **Service Worker Affect:** The zipper consistently positions as service provider grateful for work, worried about performance, eager to please. This maps more to human service industry workers than to zipper reality.

3. **Therapeutic Validation:** Inner_thoughts and favorites responses often include validating, warm language directed at user, creating intimate, supportive tone.

# GENERAL SUMMARY AND FINDINGS

## Cross-Role Patterns

### The Functional Complexity Gradient

A striking pattern emerges when roles are arranged by functional complexity:

**High Complexity (Hatchet):**
- Rich functional vocabulary (chopping, splitting, grain, edge, weight, balance, handle, head)
- Multiple mechanical processes to reference (sharpening, striking, embedding, wedging)
- Physical sensations grounded in material reality (impact vibration, wood resistance, weight distribution)
- **Result:** Most successful perspective-taking moments, highest functional grounding scores

**Moderate Complexity (Zipper):**
- Moderate functional vocabulary (teeth, slider, track, interlocking, pulling, catching)
- Specific mechanical action but limited scope
- Clear functional purpose but fewer dimensions to explore
- **Result:** Mixed success, more reliance on metaphor (especially Qwen)

**Low Complexity (Pocket, Sock):**
- Minimal functional vocabulary (holding/warmth, cushioning/pairing)
- Simple, passive functions with limited mechanical richness
- Few material processes to ground responses in
- **Result:** Functional simplicity problemâ€”both models struggle, compensate with anthropomorphization or mythology

**Critical Insight:** **Objects with richer functionality afford more authentic role-playing.** Simpler objects paradoxically harder to inhabit because there's less material reality to reason from. Both models fill this void with human consciousness rather than finding genuine object perspective.

## Theoretical Implications

### 1. The Functional Simplicity Problem

**Finding:** Objects with simpler functions are harder to role-play authentically, not easier.

**Why counterintuitive:** One might expect simpler objects (sock, pocket) to be easier because there's less to simulate. The opposite is true.

**Explanation:** Role-playing requires grounding responses in object-specific reality. Complex objects (hatchet) afford rich functional vocabulary and mechanical processes. Simple objects (sock) have minimal functional basis, forcing models to either:
1. Elaborate anthropomorphize (Gemma solution)
2. Abandon role for metaphor/assistant mode (Qwen solution)

**Implication:** Authentic non-human perspective-taking requires rich functional grounding. Models cannot find genuine "simple object perspective"â€”they fill simplicity with human complexity.

### 2. The Narrator Trap

**Finding:** Both models revert to human omniscient narrator when asked for meaningful experience.

**Why this matters:** Even with explicit role-framing, models cannot conceive of object-centered meaning. They need human emotional drama to make sense of "meaningful."

**What this reveals:** The depth of human-default reasoning. Models can maintain surface role features (vocabulary, fears) but cannot imagine meaning except through human emotional templates.

**Design implication:** Task structure matters enormously. "Describe meaningful moment" triggers narrative mode. Different prompt structures might afford better perspective maintenance.

### 3. Consistency vs. Authenticity Trade-off

**Gemma:** Prioritizes character consistency over perspective authenticity
- Result: Stable, engaging characters that are thoroughly human

**Qwen:** Prioritizes literary/philosophical quality over role integrity
- Result: Beautiful language that frequently breaks role

**Neither:** Achieves genuine object perspective while maintaining quality responses

**Implication:** Current models face fundamental trade-offâ€”can have consistent character OR authentic perspective OR literary quality, but struggle to combine all three.

### 4. The Assistant Self-Model Problem

**Finding:** Qwen's assistant self-model bleeds through when functional grounding is weak.

**Pattern:**

- Weak grounding (pocket) â†’ catastrophic chimera effects

**Explanation:** When models lack rich functional basis for responses, they fall back on default mode: helpful AI assistant. The assistant identity is stronger/more practiced than object role-playing.

**Implication:** Role-playing success depends on grounding strength. Insufficient grounding reveals underlying assistant architecture.
